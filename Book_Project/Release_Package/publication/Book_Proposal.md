# Book Proposal

**Computational Metagenomics and Modern Biomedical Omics: A Practical Handbook from 16S Analysis to Spatial and Multiomic Interpretation**

## Positioning

This book is designed as a professional handbook for readers who need a coherent path from microbiome sequencing to broader biomedical omics analysis. It integrates workshop-derived pedagogic material with publication-oriented review writing and is positioned between a classroom text and a methods reference.

## Core Promise

The book teaches readers how to move from raw sequence data to defensible biological interpretation while making the right decisions about study design, preprocessing, model choice, reproducibility, and translational restraint.

## Intended Readership

- graduate students in bioinformatics, microbiology, public health, and computational biology
- clinicians entering microbiome or omics research
- biomedical researchers expanding from one assay to multi-assay studies
- workshop organizers and course directors who need structured teaching material

## Distinctive Features

- integrates metagenomics with modern omics instead of treating them as isolated subjects
- emphasizes failure modes, reporting discipline, and publication logic
- includes command-line, workflow, glossary, and further-learning appendices
- suitable for short-course teaching, independent study, and manuscript preparation
