# Back Cover Copy

Computational biology is no longer limited to a single sequencing workflow. Researchers now move between microbiome profiling, shotgun metagenomics, genome-resolved analysis, compositional statistics, host-associated interpretation, genomics, transcriptomics, multiomics integration, single-cell data, spatial assays, and emerging dynamic tissue-resolved technologies. What remains rare is one book that explains how these domains connect and how to reason through them professionally.

**Computational Metagenomics and Modern Biomedical Omics** provides that roadmap. Built from a complete training and manuscript-development program, this handbook translates scattered methods into one coherent analytical framework. It emphasizes study design, preprocessing, model choice, interpretation, reproducibility, reporting standards, and translational caution.

This book is written for:

- microbiome and omics researchers
- clinicians and physician-scientists
- postgraduate students and trainees
- workshop instructors and course designers
- data analysts moving into biomedical sequencing

Rather than offering a catalog of software names, it shows how to make defensible analytical decisions from raw reads to publication-ready conclusions.
