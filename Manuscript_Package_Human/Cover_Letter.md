Dear Editor,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript, **"Human Computational Metagenomics: A Practical Roadmap for Host-Associated Microbiome Analysis from Raw Reads to Clinical Interpretation,"** for publication as a tutorial review in *Briefings in Bioinformatics*.

This manuscript addresses a specific methodological gap in the microbiome literature. While many tutorial articles discuss general computational metagenomics, fewer provide a focused roadmap for host-associated human studies, where the analysis must also account for heavy host DNA background, contamination risk in low-biomass specimens, clinical covariates, repeated measures, privacy-sensitive sequence carryover, and translational interpretation.

The article presents a structured and publication-oriented guide to study design, quality control, host-read subtraction, taxonomic and functional profiling, genome-resolved analysis, statistical modeling, contamination audit, ethics, and reporting standards for human-associated microbiome research. It is intended as an educational and methodological synthesis rather than as a report of a new patient dataset. The figures are clearly labeled as tutorial schematics and illustrative examples.

I confirm that this manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration elsewhere. I am the sole author, have approved the submitted version, and declare no competing interests. No external funding was declared for preparation of this manuscript.
